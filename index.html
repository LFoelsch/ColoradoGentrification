Redlining and Gentrification in Denver, CO

This map displays three variables over all populated Census tract in Denver, CO. All values represent the percent change of that variable in that tract from the year 2000 to the year 2015.

  • The first variable, POPCHNG, represents the percent change in total population of the tract from 2000-2015. Gentrified areas tend to exhibit an increase in overall population.
  • The second variable, PCTNWCHG, is the percent change in the Non-Hispanic White population from 2000 to 2015 as reported by the U.S. Census Bureau. In addition to an increase in the overall population, gentrification also has been shown to correlate with an increase in the percentage of white people living in an area.
  • The third variable, PCTRENTCHG, is the percent change in the total number of rental units in that tract from 2000 to 2015 as reported by the U.S. Census Bureau. Gentrification typically spurs an increase in development which subsequently causes the creation of new rental units. Whether or not the units are affordable for the population at risk of displacement from the gentrificaiton is another question.
